Common Questions

Before You Write

Do you accept unsolicited article pitches?

Yes — selectively. We welcome pitches from experienced writers with clear, original angles on economics, markets, business, or finance. Please read at least three of our existing articles before pitching so your submission reflects our editorial voice and standards. A 150–200 word pitch is ideal.

Is Money and Meaning open to sponsored content?

No. We do not publish sponsored articles, native advertising, or paid editorial of any kind. Our independence is the foundation of our editorial credibility. If you would like to reach our audience, please look elsewhere — we aren't the right fit.

I found a factual error. What should I do?

Please contact us immediately using the form above or by emailing hello@moneyandmeaning.com. We take accuracy seriously. If an error is confirmed, we issue a correction prominently on the original article and acknowledge the reader who flagged it.

Can I republish or translate a Money and Meaning article?

Please contact us before republishing or translating. We consider each request individually. Non-commercial republication with full attribution and a link to the original is sometimes permitted. Commercial use always requires explicit written permission.

Is any of your content financial advice?

No. Everything published on Money and Meaning is editorial and educational content only. Nothing we publish constitutes personalised financial, investment, tax, or legal advice. Always consult a qualified professional before making any financial decision.

How do I manage or cancel my newsletter subscription?

Every newsletter email includes a one-click unsubscribe link at the bottom. You can also email us directly and we'll remove you from our list immediately. We do not make unsubscribing difficult — ever.
